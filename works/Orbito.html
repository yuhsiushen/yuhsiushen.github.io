Orbito

Project brief

Placed around famous historical landmarks, Orbito will detect when a tourist is approaching and with Lorawan technology will emit custom options on the signs based on their personal preferences.

Role

Coding, Circuit making, Design thinking, Scenario, Prototype making, Presentation

Client

A2A Smart City Milano

Time

Nov. 2017 ＠Domus Academy

Team

Dan Deng, Nancy Kumar, Yasamin Givechi 

Research Phases

Key words

Focus on heritage-based development remains central to smart urban planning, boosting the tourist-based economies while assuring that the local historical heritage is preserved.

Proposal

Using LoraWan Technology, we wanted to find a way to help bring real time, filtered informative guide to tourists surrounding various events or notable points of interest happening around the city.

We want to make it more convenient, enticing, or both for tourists to explore more of a city or country by enabling them to go off the traditional beaten path without having to think or look up a location. We vision a better type of smart city for travelers.

Concept

Concept

A modern directional guide will be placed in areas with historical or cultural significance. The guide comes with a twist - Tourists bluetooth enabled devices will send their social history data to trigger LoraWan to send personal event suggestions to the digital guide. Tourists will then see the guide rotate towards the event location with real time walking distance from their current point in time.
